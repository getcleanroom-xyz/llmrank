I've been doing SEO for 12 years and I still meet people who think Google works like a phone book. You list your business, people find you. Done.

It's more complicated than that. And less complicated than SEO agencies want you to believe.

## How Google actually ranks pages

Google's ranking system has three main stages:

**1. Crawling.** Googlebot visits pages by following links. It downloads the HTML, renders JavaScript (sometimes), and adds pages to its index. If your page isn't linked from anywhere, Google may never find it.

**2. Indexing.** Google processes the crawled content, understands what the page is about, and stores it in a massive index. Think of it as a library catalog. Each page gets categorized by topic, quality signals, and freshness.

**3. Ranking.** When someone searches, Google queries its index and ranks results using hundreds of signals. The most important ones:

- **Relevance.** Does the page answer the query? This is mostly about keywords, headers, and content matching.
- **Authority.** Is the page from a trusted source? Backlinks from other sites signal authority.
- **User experience.** Is the page fast, mobile-friendly, and easy to use?
- **Freshness.** Is the content recent? Some queries demand up-to-date results.

The algorithm is incredibly sophisticated but the core idea is simple: find the most relevant, authoritative page for a query and show it to the user.

## Why this is changing

Here's what most SEO people don't want to admit: Google's market share is eroding. Not because Google got worse, but because the user behavior shifted.

People started asking questions instead of typing keywords. "How do I fix my dishwasher" instead of "dishwasher repair near me." AI models are much better at answering questions than search engines are. A search engine gives you 10 links. An AI gives you the answer.

When someone asks ChatGPT "how do I fix my dishwasher," the model doesn't show you links. It tells you what to do. Maybe it recommends a specific tool or service. That recommendation is the new ranking.

## What this means for brands

In the Google world, you optimized for rankings. You built backlinks, wrote keyword-rich content, and competed for position #1.

In the AI world, you optimize for mentions. You get cited in authoritative sources, structure your content for comprehension, and compete to be part of the answer.

The brands that understand this shift early have a massive advantage. Most companies are still optimizing for a search engine that fewer people are using for recommendations. Meanwhile, AI models are becoming the default way people discover products and services.

You don't need to abandon SEO. But you need to start paying attention to AI visibility too. The window where you can build presence in these models because your competitors haven't bothered is measured in months, not years.
