I ran a simple test last week. I asked ChatGPT to compare three privacy tools in our space. One competitor was mentioned in every response. Another showed up occasionally. We weren't mentioned at all.

That told me everything I needed to know about where we stand.

## The competitor audit you should run

Here's the exact process:

**Step 1: Pick 15 queries.** Use the questions your customers actually ask. Don't guess — pull from your support tickets, sales calls, and keyword data. "What's the best [category] tool for [use case]?" "How does [competitor] compare to [competitor]?" "Is there a free alternative to [competitor]?"

**Step 2: Run them through ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude.** Each model has different training data and different tendencies. ChatGPT tends to favor well-established brands. Gemini pulls more from recent web data. Claude is more cautious about recommendations.

**Step 3: Record everything.** Which brands are mentioned? In what position? What sentiment? Which competitors appear together? This gives you a competitive visibility map.

**Step 4: Look for patterns.** Where does the competitor appear but you don't? Those are your blind spots. Maybe they're mentioned in review sites you're not on. Maybe they have comparison pages you don't. Maybe they're active in forums where your audience hangs out.

## What the competitor is probably doing differently

Based on what I've seen across dozens of brands, the ones that dominate AI recommendations usually have:

**More mentions in trusted sources.** They're on G2, Capterra, Reddit, and industry publications. Not just their own blog.

**Better content structure.** Their comparison pages are honest and detailed. Their "what is X" pages define the category clearly. AI models can extract and reference this content easily.

**Consistent presence over time.** They've been mentioned in the right places for months or years. The compounding effect of consistent visibility is hard to beat.

**Active community participation.** They answer questions on forums, respond to Reddit threads, and contribute to industry discussions. This creates natural, quotable mentions that AI models pick up.

## Closing the gap

You don't need to beat the competitor everywhere. You need to beat them where it matters — the queries your potential customers actually ask.

Start with the 5 queries where the competitor appears and you don't. Focus on getting mentioned in the specific sources that feed those responses. One good comparison page, three forum responses, and a review site presence can close the gap faster than you'd think.

The goal isn't to dominate every AI response. It's to be in the conversation when it matters.
